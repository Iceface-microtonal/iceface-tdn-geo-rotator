Understanding K / S / H Placement

K — KICK

The low-end pillar

Place on beats 1 and 3 (four-on-the-floor)

Add on beats 2 and 4 for more drive

Dense kicks create a house feel

S — SNARE

The backbeat anchor

Beats 2 and 4 is the standard (backbeat)

Beat 1 snare adds variation

Rolls create fills

H — HIHAT

The rhythmic subdivision

Even 8th or 16th-note patterns

Offbeat placement adds shuffle

Density controls the sense of speed

Reading the 16-Step Grid

1 bar = 16 steps (4 beats × 4 subdivisions)

Built-in Patterns

Default (12 steps)

Balanced, all-round pattern. The starting point for first-time exploration.

Four on Ice (16 steps)

Basic four-on-the-floor. A "ruler" pattern for observing T(D,N) time transformations.

Ghost Drift (16 steps)

Syncopated breakbeat. Spatial movement with velocity ripples.

Rose's Waltz (12 steps)

Polyrhythmic approach with 12 steps. Triple-meter groove.

Asymmetry (16 steps)

Irregular accents across kick and snare. Off-kilter groove.

Deep Glitch (16 steps)

Dense, overlapping hits. Chaotic, glitchy texture.

T ( D , N )   &   D R U M S

How N Affects Drum Timing

The T(D,N) N parameter changes the step interval of non-diatonic notes. This causes drum and note timing to shift relative to each other, producing a unique groove feel.

N = 1.00

Equal / Straight

All steps even. Drums and notes perfectly synced.

N = 0.75

Light Shuffle

Non-diatonic notes lag slightly behind.

N = 0.50

Iceface / Half Shuffle

50¢ pitch shift and timing change happen together. Linked with Iceface tunings.

N = 0.25

Heavy Shuffle

Large timing displacement. Experimental groove.

Tip: Selecting an Iceface tuning automatically sets N = 0.5. Try combining it with different drum patterns to explore the relationship between pitch transformation and rhythmic feel.

Compressor & Drum Filter

COMPRESSOR

COMP ON/OFFEnable/disable compressor
AMOUNTCompression ratio. Higher = more even levels
MAKEUPPost-compression volume compensation

TIPS

Amount 0.3–0.5: natural punch
Amount 0.8+: electronic clamp feel
MAKEUP 0.1–0.2 is natural

DRUM FILTER

CUTOFFCutoff frequency (rolls off high end)
RESONANCEEmphasis at cutoff point

TIPS

Low cutoff: muffled lo-fi feel
Cutoff + high resonance: acid character
Drum-only filter (does not affect synth)
